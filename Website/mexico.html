History of Mexico

Mexican-American War:

The war began when America wanted to expand westward to spread slavery at Rio Grande. President Polk wanted Texas to become an indepedent state however the Mexican people would not acknowledge them as a state especially since this land belonged to Mexico. Eventually, Polk claimed shots were fired and Mexico went into war with America. At the end, America won and gained control of areas now known as California, Nevada, New Mexico, Arizona, etc.

Previously, the border was open and anyone was allowed to pass through with inspection. After the Mexican Revolution, America soon joined World War I, where there was a sudden fear of the Mexican border. During this era, there was also the idea of eugenics, the idea of the best race and cleansing those who were not superior. Because of this, Mexican communities such as El Paso were targetted to ensure they did not have Typhus (which is spread through lice). Additionally, those who did have lice, they were forced to burn clothes, sent to jail and soon all immigrants had to have their clothes disinfected as well as check their bodies. These inspections of the body for lice happened every eight days when passing the border.
